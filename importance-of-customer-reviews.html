The Importance of Customer Reviews (New Data)

by Ken McMahon

Do online reviews move the needle? The importance of customer reviews couldn’t be higher. It affects your acquisition, account retention, and brand.

Customer reviews are one of your most powerful selling tools as a business, but they don’t just happen.

While unhappy customers are quick to turn to review sites to voice their concerns, happy and loyal customers may not think twice about sharing their incredible experiences — and those are the reviews that potential clients need to see.

This post discusses why customer reviews are so important for reputation management. We’ll also share how to generate and manage reviews that can help your business.

Types of Customer Reviews

There are several different types of customer reviews, including:

  • Ratings: Ratings are typically given a one-to-five-star ranking and often include a brief explanation. This is the most common type of customer review.
  • Quotes & Testimonials: Testimonials are often featured prominently in a brand’s marketing, either on their site or on social media channels. They can be written or include multimedia elements like images or videos. They’re almost always positive, and they may be part of a customer case study.
  • Videos: Video reviews typically feature interviews with happy customers. Customers and influencers alike may shoot videos showing how they use a product or service and their overall experience working with the company.
  • Aggregators: Aggregator sites collect reviews from different customers for a single product or service, bringing them all together in one place. Popular examples of aggregate review sites for B2B and SaaS software products are G2 and Capterra.

You can benefit from having multiple types of customer reviews online, including ones that are published on third-party sites and review platforms.

The Impact of Customer Reviews

The importance of customer reviews can’t be overstated; they are incredibly powerful social proof that your product or service delivers on its promises. Let’s discuss the main benefits of consumer reviews.

Build trust and create brand credibility in the marketplace

Every business can claim that customer reviews offer a great solution. But when customers themselves say in their own words that a tool is valuable, reliable, and effective, it’s much more powerful.

It’s plain and simple: Online reviews help create trust. This is reflected in many customer service statistics.

  • 76% of people “always” or “regularly” read online reviews for local businesses (BrightLocal)
  • Featuring customer reviews on a landing page can increase conversion rates by up to 270% (Spiegel Research)

Reviews are actually trusted 12 times more than other marketing materials, and some consumers trust online reviews as much as word-of-mouth recommendations. This can help with both brand awareness and brand credibility, especially in crowded markets.

Both positive reviews and negative reviews can make an impact, with 52% of buyers saying they actually trust a brand more if there are a few negative reviews. For best results, leave the negative reviews up and address the reviewer, offering to find a solution.

Shorten the sales cycle

When buyers read reviews, they often feel more confident in their purchasing decisions. This is part social proof and part trust, but either way, it can accelerate the speed at which customers move through your pipeline.

Keep in mind that 89% of users will make a purchase within one week after researching reviews, and 68% of users read reviews while building their shortlist during the research stage of the typical buyer’s journey.

Increase conversion rates and revenue

There’s plenty of data showing that online customer reviews can significantly increase sales. This is partially thanks to the increases in trust and brand awareness we’ve already discussed.

  • Products are up to 270% more likely to sell with even as little as five reviews
  • Customers spend an average of 31% more with businesses that have great reviews

Strengthen social proof

Social proof can make a big impact on how customers perceive your brand. If you have a ton of great reviews, potential clients are going to take note. After all, your prospective customers want to be happy customers!

Reviews are particularly valuable as social proof when they stress how well your solutions worked for their specific needs. Reviews from consumers in different industries or customer segments can help potential customers see that you can help clients just like them.

Boost search visibility

Plenty of reviews can help boost your ranking in search engine results pages (SERPs), so it’s a vital part of SEO efforts.

Local search is often dominated by businesses with high star ratings, and businesses with the highest number of reviews are given priority. Therefore, generating as many positive reviews as possible can help you attract more clicks.

This concept holds true on other third-party platforms outside of Google, too. Business profiles on social media or review sites with more consumer reviews often show up higher in a search, increasing your overall visibility as well.

Collect genuine customer feedback

Buyers don’t always provide transparent feedback to their account representatives. Regularly collecting reviews is an opportunity to gather customer feedback that can not only help you attract more customers but also help improve your product and customer experience overall.

Lower acquisition costs

In many cases, customer reviews can accelerate the customer journey. As the decision-making process is streamlined, businesses spend less time nurturing leads. They also spend less money attracting leads when potential customers can find them easily during the research process.

This results in lower overall customer acquisition costs, increasing your ROI and revenue.

Tactics to Get Genuine Customer Reviews

Business owners and marketing teams alike want more customer reviews, and there are a few foolproof tactics you can use to get them at scale.

  • Create a coherent customer review strategy: Determine where in the buyer’s journey you’ll ask for reviews and what messaging you’ll use. You should also consider which review sites you want to drive traffic to and whether you want to ask for reviews through business texting or email.
  • Make it easy: If you want more business reviews, make it easy for customers to write them. In your messaging requesting testimonials, provide a link that automatically opens to where users will leave a review. You should also have prominent review sections, functions, and links on your website or app so customers can easily access them anytime.
  • Manage reviews carefully: Review management software makes it easy for you to assess and manage incoming reviews. It may also have features to display new reviews on your site, even if they come from third-party platforms.
  • Incentivize reviews: When customers leave reviews on your website, offer them incentives like tickets, vouchers, gift cards, or discounts. You can also enter users into a giveaway if they leave a review. Remember, though, that most third-party platforms do not allow this.
  • Use existing relationships: If someone on your team, either a salesperson or an account manager, has worked directly with the customer, have them ask personally for a review. Let them know how much it helps your brand, and in many cases, you’ll see participation increase.
  • Create a presence on popular review sites: Ensure you have a review section enabled on social media profiles like Google, Facebook, and LinkedIn. You also want to create profiles on high-value review sites relevant to your customer segments; typically, these will be industry-relevant reviews.
  • Act on what customers are saying: If you want more reviews in the future, show that you pay attention to the ones you already have. Interact with reviews when the platform allows it. Thank users for positive reviews, and address concerns in negative reviews. Take feedback under consideration and let them know you passed it along to the team.
  • Get the timing right: Timing matters when you’re trying to collect reviews. Software or service-based companies typically benefit from asking for customer reviews within a day after a purchase is made. Meanwhile, consumers are more likely to leave reviews for eCommerce brands after they’ve had time to use a product, so a few days to a week after the customer receives the item is best.

To get the most out of your reviews, feature them prominently. Feature client quotes and particularly powerful testimonials on your homepage, and place relevant reviews on individual product pages.

You also want to consider how the reviews are displayed. Seth Godin recently wrote about how Amazon is changing its review display formatting and how that can impact customer perception.

In short, if you’re putting the customer experience first, you’re off to a good start.

Top Customer Review Websites to Track

In addition to featuring reviews on your own website, having profiles on relevant customer review platforms can be a great way to expand your reach and boost visibility.

Amazon Customer Reviews: These reviews are visible on products listed on the massive eCommerce marketplace. Many customers will turn to Amazon to find and research products (including reading reviews) even if they purchase elsewhere.

AppExchange: Owned by Salesforce, this marketplace allows users to review enterprise software solutions. Since Salesforce is a trusted name, these reviews carry a lot of weight.

Better Business Bureau: This consumer advocacy organization provides information and recommendations about member companies. Businesses in all industries can have profiles here, and customers often use the platform to research small businesses they are considering.

Capterra: Great for finding comprehensive and verified software reviews. This site has vetted over 675,000 verified user reviews to date. They have both professional and customer reviews.

G2: An online peer-to-peer review platform for business software solutions, G2 is best used for side-by-side comparisons of competing solutions. It’s one of the more popular review sites.

Consumer Reports: A magazine and nonprofit organization that predates the Internet by several decades, it publishes unbiased product tests and comprehensive, well-presented reviews for any consumer product.

Edmunds: An online review and rating site focusing on automobiles, this is the site most consumers use to research the value and safety of different car makes and models.

Facebook Ratings: This is a way to gather and display brand ratings on the world’s largest social network. Make sure you enable the review tab on your business profile.

Google Maps and Search: A service by the planet’s dominant search engine for businesses, Google Reviews bulk up your rankings with complete contact information and user ratings.

Home Advisor: This is an online platform for reviews of home services and products targeting residential customers. You can find home contractors, landscapers, handymen, and more.

Software Advice: A company aiming to help customers make the best purchasing decisions when it comes to software, Software Advice provides detailed reviews and product comparisons.

TripAdvisor: This website provides user reviews and recommendations on all things travel and tourism. It’s long been established as a premier review site.

Yelp: A local search and ratings platform, Yelp facilitates crowd-sourced reviews of local services. This could include restaurants, plumbing or electrical companies, and medical services. Reviews focus on the businesses more than individual products.

Zomato: A search and discovery service focusing on restaurants, cafes, food, and deliveries, this platform features user-generated ratings. Its solid traffic means it’s used regularly by diners.
